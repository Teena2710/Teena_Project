Kerala Transport and Vehicle Registration Process in Kerala

The Kerala Transport Motor Vehicles Department has been established to bring into effect the rules, regulations, and provisions made under the Central Motor Vehicles Act of 1988. The department undertakes the development and modernisation of road transport and related procedures in the state for better convenience of the citizens. It provides services such as vehicle registration, issue of vehicle fitness certificates, two wheeler insurance and driving licenses, and collection of taxes and fines to contribute to the revenue of the state.

Importance of Vehicle Registration

Registration of motor vehicles is mandatory in India as per the Central Motor Vehicles Act of 1988. The act prohibits vehicle owners whose vehicles are not register from driving vehicles in any public place in India. Therefore, it is of great importance to get your vehicle registered in order to avoid any legal troubles.

Documents Required For Vehicle Registration

To register a vehicle in Kerala, an application in Form 20 has to be made to the registering authority along with other necessary documents. The application for vehicle registration should be made within a period of 7 days from the date of vehicle purchase
1.Duly filled application Form No.20
2.Vehicle Sales Certificate in Form 21
3.Form 22 supplied by vehicle seller
4.Insurance paper of the vehicle
5.PUC Certificate
6.Passport sized photos
7.Proof of address
8.Temporary registration certificate (if any)
9.Form A (Taxation Act, 1997)
10.Certificate of custom clearance (In case of imported vehicles)
11.Vehicle needs to be presented for physical inspection
12.Applicable fee
